# PuffSat Pusher-Plate Restitution Simulation — Design Document

**Status:** Draft for review
**Date:** 2026-06-16
**Author context:** Seth Katz (paper author). Design distilled from a grilling session on how to simulate the energy-loss "fudge factor" `f` in *Aim Is All You Need: A Speculative White Paper on PuffSat Pulsed Propulsion*.
**Intended home:** a separate implementation repository (not this LaTeX paper repo). Companion to `puffsat_control_sim_design.md`; the two share only the vehicle, not the physics.
**Backs:** paper §3.2 ("Radiative Differences From Project Orion"), which currently states "detailed computer analysis is needed for full confirmation." This sim is that analysis.

---

## 1. Purpose

Compute one quantity: the **per-collision coefficient of restitution** of the PuffSat gas against the pusher plate, expressed as the paper's fudge factor

```
f = eta_capture * (1 + e_eff) / 2
```

as a function of relative impact speed across the mission envelope. `eta_capture` is the fraction of axial momentum that lands on the plate and rebounds usefully (geometry). `e_eff` is the effective restitution that survives radiative, conductive, and condensation losses (thermophysics).

The paper's mass-ratio law `m_r/m_p = 2f / ln((v_p - v_ri)/(v_p - v_rf))` (Appendix, eq. PuffSat_ratio) currently assumes a single constant `f = 0.8`. This sim replaces that assumption with a defensible `f(v)`.

**This is a per-collision study, not a performance study.** The result is `f(v)` and its loss budget. How those pulses are then smoothed into vehicle acceleration (the buffer invariant) is explicitly out of scope (§9).

## 2. Reference case

| Quantity | Value | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Pulse mass | 25 kg | gas delivered per PuffSat |
| Plate radius | <= 5 m | sets footprint, eta_capture, column density |
| Plate mass | 3-4 t | the **plate**, not the vehicle; only used to confirm rigid-wall (§5) |
| Impact speed | 3.2 to 16 km/s | swept; see §3 |
| Baseline gas | water (H2O) | covers icy/off-world PuffSats; see §4 |
| Chemical energy | **0** | conservative; see §4 |

Velocity envelope reasoning: during LEO insertion the target accelerates, so the relative impact speed falls from ~11 km/s toward ~3.2 km/s (the target reaches ~7.8 km/s orbital). Decelerating shots can close at up to ~16 km/s. Near-Sun work (hundreds of km/s) uses solid projectiles and a different chamber; it is a separate study (§9).

## 3. The physics that shaped the design

A scaling pass fixed the regime before any tool was chosen. The numbers below are order-of-magnitude and drove the decisions; they are not the deliverable.

**Bulk motion is the energy source, not the chemistry.** At 11 km/s the specific kinetic energy is 60.5 MJ/kg; total 1.5 GJ for a 25 kg pulse, about 13x the chemical energy of the same mass of TNT. So composition matters for the gas *species* (EOS, opacity, condensation), not for the energy budget. We do not model detonation; we model a cold gas arriving at `v`.

**The collision crosses three regimes across the velocity envelope:**

| v (km/s) | KE (MJ/kg) | KE/chem | T (naive cap) | Regime |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 3.2 | 5.1 | 1.3 | < 1700 K | cool neutral gas, **optically thin** |
| 6.0 | 18 | 4.5 | ~6000 K | dissociating / partially ionized |
| 11 | 60 | 15 | ~20 kK | strong plasma, optically thick |
| 16 | 128 | 32 | ~43 kK | strong plasma, optically thick |

**At high speed, opacity is "won" with margin.** At plausible cloud densities (0.16-0.64 kg/m^3) the stagnated plasma is deeply optically thick (tau ~ 1e2 to 1e5 even on a pessimistic free-free estimate). Project Orion's opacity argument holds. Radiation does not free-stream into the plate; it diffuses slowly. So `e_eff` is set mainly by gas dynamics and geometry, not by radiative loss, and "ensuring a good bounce" is the dominant lever precisely because opacity is favorable. Radiation re-enters only as the survivability constraint.

**At low speed the opacity argument fails, and a different one takes over.** At 3.2 km/s the stagnated gas is ~1700 K, neutral, and optically thin. But it is so cold it radiates almost nothing (flux scales ~v^8, so ~5e-5 of the 11 km/s level). The low-v bounce is governed by classical gas-dynamic re-expansion minus condensation/recombination loss. With chemical energy zeroed, the hypothesis was that this is the **worst case in the whole study: water at 3.2 km/s** — if it clears a useful `f`, every other case is easier. **The 1D model refutes the strong form of this (Rung C + B-flux, §10):** 3.2 km/s clears a comfortable `e_eff ≈ 0.74` but is *not* the floor — it bounces *better* than 16 km/s (0.63), because both its loss channels are starved (radiation ~v^8-negligible; condensation/conduction starved by the ~µs bounce). So "if 3.2 km/s clears `f`, everything else does too" does not hold. The worst case is not assumed at an endpoint but **located by measurement at the transitional dip** (ADR-0012, which already flagged the `τ~1` transition as the likely true minimum and the least-certain anchor). **The dense sweep finds it (§10 Transitional anchor):** the EOS-only (opacity-independent) `e_eff(v)` has a minimum ≈ 0.57 near 11 km/s — below both endpoints — driven by energy sinking into dissociation/ionization. That EOS floor is trustworthy now; the separate *radiative* `τ~1` leak dip awaits the real opacity table (the interim Kramers opacity is structurally wrong at `τ~1`), so ≈ 0.57 is a lower bound on `f`'s EOS side that the real-opacity rung can only push deeper.

**The survivability-defining case is the opposite end: 16 km/s.** Ram pressure scales as v^2 (2.1x the 11 km/s value) and radiative flux as ~v^8 (~20x). A disk-like shot at 16 km/s is ~200 MPa stagnation pressure (measured reflected-shock coefficient ≈ 1.2·rho·v², 2026-07 correction — see §7). So the worst case for `f` and the worst case for survivability sit at opposite ends of the velocity range.

## 4. Gas model (settled)

- **Baseline gas: water.** This covers the icy and LOX off-world PuffSats directly, and with chemical energy zeroed it also covers terrestrial explosive PuffSats, so one `f(v)` curve serves all three. Water is also the conservative choice for restitution: it re-condenses readily (latent heat 2.26 MJ/kg), and condensed mass that deposits at the wall sticks instead of bouncing, which is the dominant low-v loss now that chemistry is gone.
- **Confirmatory spot-checks:** oxygen (LOX) and an ANFO-product mix (N2/H2O/CO2/CO), run at the optimum rather than swept, and compared at the *water* optimum's cloud conditions for an apples-to-apples species test. Each gets a full pipeline re-run (ADR-0007) scoped to the optimum's `(rho,T)` neighborhood — never water-scaled, which would assume the answer. O2 is a trivial single-species re-run; the ANFO mix leans on CEA's native multi-species equilibrium for EOS/composition, with opacity built as the equilibrium-composition-weighted sum of per-component spectral opacities (summed spectrally, then mean-ed). Water is expected to stay the conservative baseline (heavier species run cooler → less radiation; H2O condenses most). See ADR-0017.
- **Chemical/explosion energy set to zero in the bounce.** The chemistry still gasifies the cloud (it sets the cloud-at-impact state) but contributes nothing to the rebound. Consequence: `f <= 1` strictly. The pessimism is a ~40% energy haircut at 3.2 km/s (where KE ~= chemical energy) and negligible above ~8 km/s (6% at 11, 3% at 16). This is the price of one curve valid for explosive and icy PuffSats alike.
- **EOS:** equilibrium with dissociation and ionization (Saha / CEA-style). An ideal-gamma EOS is wrong here because it skips the ionization enthalpy that buffers the temperature (the paper's §3.2 "higher specific heat capacity" point) and so over-predicts T and the ~T^4 flux.
- **Opacity:** real tabulated opacities for the actual species, **not Kramers.** Kramers free-free assumes full ionization and is orders of magnitude wrong at the 10-20 kK partial-ionization conditions. The survivability flux that defines the frontier rides entirely on getting this right. Two means are tabulated and used in their correct regimes — **Rosseland** in the diffusion coefficient (optically thick, high-v), **Planck** in the emission source (optically thin, low-v) — with a **Levermore-Pomraning** flux limiter spanning the two. Gray is the baseline; multigroup is gated on whether the emergent-flux spectrum matters (e.g. band-selective ablator absorption). See ADR-0006.

## 5. Plate model (settled)

**Construction: a three-layer stack.**

1. **Sacrificial ablator** (silicone or ionic liquid), renewed each pulse by space-qualified inkjet-style MEMS (paper §4.x): vapor-shields and self-heals the surface.
2. **SiC-based hot face** (CVD or monolithic SiC; C/SiC composite as an alternative), permanent: oxidation resistance plus high-temperature strength. This is the layer that meets the gas.
3. **Ductile metallic backing (titanium)**: carries the impulse into the shock absorber and gives fracture toughness so the brittle ceramic cannot spall catastrophically. It must stay below ~700 K, so it sits behind the ceramic and ablator and never sees the hot face directly. Its lower acoustic impedance than SiC means the compressive shock partly reflects at the bond as tension back into the ceramic (`R ~= -0.15`), but the backing converts what would be a free-surface full reflection (prompt spall) into only ~15%, and the graded interlayer smooths it further; the reflected tension is sub-spall at baseline loads. This SiC-Ti spall reflection is a Phase-2 structural task fed by the solver's peak-load history, decoupled from the ms-scale shock absorber. See ADR-0011.

**Why SiC, not carbon-carbon.** Every PuffSat gas we care about is oxidizing: LOX directly, and water and CO2 both dissociate to atomic oxygen at impact temperatures. Bare carbon-carbon burns (C + O -> CO/CO2) and is consumed. SiC passivates, growing a protective SiO2 layer, and survives. So C-C is out as the hot face for our chemistry. This sharpens what the paper already gestures at (§4.x: oxygen attacking PICA phenolic, adding B4C/HfC; and the LOX "non-combustible lining" note). The real engineering risk in the stack is the SiC-Ti bond: their thermal expansion differs by ~2x (SiC ~4 ppm/K, Ti ~9), so the join needs a graded interlayer or compliant braze. That is a Phase-2 structural and thermal-shock task, not a gas-dynamics one.

**The damage regime is thermal/chemical, not atomic.** Across the envelope the gas atoms carry only ~1-21 eV each (heaviest species, oxygen, at 16 km/s), at or below the SiC lattice displacement threshold (~20-35 eV) and ~1000x below Orion's ~10 keV tungsten ions. So the atoms cannot crystal-damage the SiC; they have sub-nm range and are stopped in the top of the ablator regardless. The SiC threats are oxidation (chemical) and thermal shock (structural), both addressed above. A few-micron ablator therefore stops the atoms with large margin; its binding thickness constraint is thermal burn-through per pulse, not penetration. Displacement/sputtering damage is out of scope (§11). See ADR-0010.

**Rigid wall is a good approximation.** A 3-4 t plate recoils only `2*m_gas/M_plate` ~= 1.4% of the impact speed per pulse (velocity-independent, ~230 m/s at 16 km/s). That 1.4% is the entire error in the rigid-wall assumption. We start rigid (Phase 1) and add the ablating, transpiring wall (Phase 2) for the real frontier. That wall is *passive* (the MEMS-renewed sacrificial layer vaporizes and blows into the boundary layer; not pumped coolant) and, counter to intuition, tends to *raise* `e_eff`: blowing and vapor shielding cut the conductive and radiative wall losses faster than the small (~1.5% of pulse mass) ablation enthalpy costs. So rigid is the conservative floor and the ablating wall is the best-estimate refinement (ADR-0014). If the 1% recoil correction is ever wanted, the 1D solver takes the plate as a heavy piston for free.

**Plate structural body (Phase-2 mass-optimization).** Getting a 2-4 t, 5 m-radius body stiff and strong enough to carry the integrated impulse to the shock absorber is a structural design distinct from the local facesheet. A Ti truss/corrugated core with a tensioned high-strength-fiber (e.g. Vectran) tension back-face is a candidate, far more mass-efficient in bending than solid Ti (stiffness ~ depth^2). Two constraints bind it: (1) per ADR-0011, no voids may sit directly behind the SiC — a continuous solid Ti spall/impedance layer comes first, and the truss/membrane begins only behind it; (2) the body's first vibrational-mode period must stay >> the pulse duration (~hundreds of us) so the face is locally rigid during the bounce, preserving the rigid-wall assumption and e_eff. Vectran is thermally limited (polymer; keep cool, far from the hot face) and needs a cyclic-fatigue check over the mission pulse count. This is fed by the solver's peak-load/impulse/footprint outputs and does not change f(v) — a parallel Phase-2 track.

**Plate shape: model flat and shallow concave (depth/diameter ~0.1-0.15).** Flat is the conservative `eta_capture` floor (hemispherical rebound, large sideways loss). Shallow concave re-collimates the rebound toward the axis — best understood as the *recovery lever* for the rebound axiality that the survivability-driven cloud stretch forces you to surrender (the disk↔cylinder `L/D` trade pits `eta_capture` against peak pressure), so its `eta_capture` value peaks at the 16 km/s anchor (§7). A deep dish would re-collimate most strongly but is **foreclosed exactly there**: its focal hot spot concentrates the rebound in the strongest-radiating, `τ≫1` gas and couples radiation into the otherwise radiation-free 2D track (ADR-0003/0008). So it stays an unrun upper-bound note, *not* a high-v contingency — where shallow concave + the cloud schedule miss the target, `eta_capture` is reported below 1 and the resulting `f` carried honestly by the dual curve (ADR-0013). See ADR-0021.

**Transient pressure-limit table** (engineering placeholders; impulsive load with thermal shock):

| Construction | Transient limit | Note |
|---|---|---|
| ablator / light substrate | 50-150 MPa | floor, non-viable |
| carbon-carbon face | 150-300 MPa | **oxidizes -> out** |
| C/SiC composite face | 200-400 MPa | tough; fibers vulnerable if matrix cracks |
| **SiC (CVD/monolithic) + Ti** | **400-700 MPa** | baseline; best oxidation; Ti carries load |
| UHTC (HfC/ZrC/HfB2-SiC) + Ti | 600-900 MPa | highest T and oxidation; heavy, costly |

At 16 km/s a stretched pulse is ~100 MPa and a disk-like one ~393 MPa, so the design is feasible across C/SiC and up. We are above the feasibility threshold, not fighting to clear it.

**Pressure-limit sensitivity (prediction to confirm, not a result).** Optimizing the pressure limit is predicted to be **low leverage** on `e_eff`, closer to "+5%, not worth it" than "doubles it," for three reasons: (1) `e_eff`'s radiative trapping is set by *column* density (mass / footprint area) and opacity, not by volumetric density or pressure, so a stronger plate only lets you run denser/sharper at a fixed footprint, which barely moves the column density; (2) past the tau >> 1 knee the residual loss is condensation and conduction, which pressure does not fix; (3) the local pressure is the only structural limit in scope, and even it sits above the feasibility threshold. **Decision rule:** baseline SiC+Ti for oxidation and feasibility margin, sweep `e_eff(P_limit)` and `f(P_limit)` at the 16 km/s anchor to find the knee, and push to UHTC only if the sweep shows a steep gain past SiC+Ti (doubted). The genuine `e_eff` levers are the dark-oil opacity boost (§7) and the footprint/curvature, not facesheet strength.

**Toughness/durability upgrade path: Si3N4 / SiAlON, not UHTC.** The transient `P_limit` is a brittle-fracture/thermal-shock allowable (in the ballpark of SiC's ~400-600 MPa flexural strength), an order of magnitude below SiC's ~3 GPa compressive crush strength — so the binding mode is tensile fracture at flaws/thermal gradients, and the property that raises it is fracture toughness + thermal-shock resistance, not compressive strength (SiC is nowhere near crushing at 400 MPa). UHTC (table above) escalates *strength and temperature* at a steep mass+cost penalty (heavy borides/carbides, 2-4.5x SiC's density) for a payoff this section predicts is low-leverage on `e_eff`. The cheaper same-family escalation is **silicon nitride / SiAlON**: comparable density (~3.2 g/cc) and the same protective SiO2 passivation as SiC, but ~2x the fracture toughness and better thermal-shock resistance. It does not raise `f` (P_limit is low-leverage), so it is reserved for the **multi-pulse cyclic-fatigue / spall-accumulation** durability axis (out of scope, §11) rather than the per-collision deliverable — the durability counterpart to UHTC's strength escalation. The hot-face baseline stays SiC+Ti.

## 6. The fudge factor and its loss budget

```
f = eta_capture * (1 + e_eff) / 2
```

- `eta_capture` (geometry): axial-momentum fraction that lands and rebounds usefully. Set by plate radius, curvature, and cloud footprint/divergence. Computed by the 2D Euler track (§7).
- `e_eff` (thermophysics): `1 - (radiative + conductive + condensation losses)`. Computed by the 1D rad-hydro track (§7).

**Explicit output: the loss decomposition.** `(1 - f)` is reported split into five channels, per velocity anchor (ADR-0016):

1a. radiative-to-plate (the **optical** fraction headed at the wall — called out as a headline number; recoverable only by the ablating-wall vapor shield, ADR-0014),
1b. radiative-escape-to-space (the optical fraction headed outward through the cooler outer gas to the open far boundary; the only radiative loss the **soot seed** can recover, ADR-0015/0016),
2. conductive-to-plate,
3. condensation / recombination (mass that sticks),
4. sideways escape / non-capture (geometric, the 2D `eta_capture` track).

The radiative channel is split because the two recovery levers act on physically distinct halves: ablation vapor shielding sits at the wall and recovers (1a); the bulk soot seed sits in the cooler outer gas and recovers (1b). Each covers the half the other physically cannot (ADR-0016). Channels 1a and 2 are set by the 1D wall boundary condition: a cold black absorber for radiation (absorptivity ~= 1, re-emission negligible during the pulse) and a semi-infinite conducting solid for conduction (loss computed self-consistently from the facesheet effusivity, interface temperature emergent). See ADR-0005. Channel 1b is set by the 1D far (non-wall) boundary, the surface the gas re-expands into.

Expectation: the optical fractions (1a + 1b) are largest at 16 km/s (but trapped by tau >> 1) and small at 3.2 km/s (cold gas barely radiates; the low-v loss is condensation- and conduction-dominated). Reporting them split is what lets us decide which lever, if either, is worth pulling: the soot seed is gated on (1b) being a meaningful slice of the deficit, not the lumped optical fraction (§7).

`f = 0.8` from the paper, for reference, implies e.g. `eta_capture ~ 0.95` and `e_eff ~ 0.68`. The sim's job is to put real numbers on both, across `v`.

**The `f = 0.8` gate.** The paper's assumed value doubles as the decision gate: if the *conservative* (equilibrium) baseline already clears `f >= 0.8` at an anchor, that case is settled and the recovery levers (kinetic condensation, dark-oil seed) are not built; below `0.8`, the levers are pulled to claw back and the residual is reported. `0.8` is a reference anchor — the existing claim this analysis defends — explicitly not a mission requirement (performance is out of scope, §11). `f(v)` is always reported with its linear mass-ratio sensitivity. See ADR-0009.

## 7. Velocity sweep, design variables, and the opacity-seed study

**Two physics packages, anchored at ~3 velocities** (3.2 / ~8 / 16 km/s):

- **Low-v package:** cool water vapor, optically thin, condensation/recombination loss, no chemistry. The condensation model is the key piece, since it is the dominant low-v sink. It splits into two channels — bulk vapor-pressure collapse (two-phase equilibrium EOS, latent heat returned to the gas, condensate co-moves and can rebound) and wall deposition (sticking BC, mass + latent heat lost to the wall). Equilibrium condensation is the conservative baseline; the kinetic nucleation/growth model is gated behind a useful-`f` threshold, since kinetic inhibition can only raise `e_eff`. See ADR-0004.
- **High-v package:** water plasma, equilibrium EOS, real opacity tables, flux-limited radiation diffusion.
- **Transitional anchor(s) (velocity located by measurement; partial-ionization window ~6-10 km/s):** modeled with the high-v package (it has the ionization EOS and plasma opacity the low-v package lacks), but flagged as the **least-certain** anchor: it sits near `tau ~ 1`, where flux-limited diffusion is weakest, and at partial ionization, where opacity is hardest and the molecular<->plasma table seam (ADR-0007) falls. `tau` is computed from the real tables there, not assumed. If `tau ~ 1`, a transport-level check (Quokka M1 or an Sn/short-characteristics solve) is deployed *here specifically* — not at the `tau >> 1` high-v end where FLD is already exact — and the transitional `f` is reported with radiation-model error bars. Velocity is swept densely (~5-9 km/s) around the transition because `e_eff` may have a **local minimum** there: hot enough to radiate strongly, thin enough (`tau ~ 1`) for that radiation to escape, unlike the cold low-v end or the trapped high-v end. The number of transitional anchors follows the data: one by default, two if the sweep reveals distinct `tau~1` and dissociation/ionization features at different velocities. See ADR-0012.

The opacity argument (Orion / §3.2) justifies only the high-v end. The low-v end rests on a separate argument: a cold gas radiates little, so little energy is lost radiatively regardless of opacity, and the bounce is gas-dynamic minus condensation.

**Plate fixed, cloud scheduled.** The plate is one built object (curvature fixed, optimized once). The cloud shape can vary per shot, so it is a schedule `shape(v)`: stretch hard at 16 km/s to survive, run sharper/denser at 3.2 km/s to beat condensation.

**Design variables (sweep primitive = the cloud-at-impact state, not deployment hardware):**

- `rho_impact` (impact density). There is no independent "impact pressure" knob: a coasting cloud is internally cold, so the plate feels the ram pressure `rho*v^2` (measured ~1.2x at stagnation, the reflected-shock `(gamma_eff+1)/2` — 2026-07 correction, ADR-0010). The optimal density is sandwiched between an opacity floor (need tau >~ a few; already met at ~0.16 kg/m^3) and a survivability ceiling, and within that band you want the lowest density that keeps tau >> 1 (the longest survivable pulse). Past the tau >> 1 knee, more density barely helps `e_eff`.
- `L/D` (the disk <-> cylinder axis).
- footprint coverage `r_foot / R`.
- radial divergence.
- plate curvature (flat, shallow).

Standoff, initial size, and detonation energy are backed out afterward; they are not modeled.

**Sweep grid (coarse-then-refine; factored, so never a full factorial).** Because the architecture factors into two tracks (ADR-0003), each sweeps only its own variables and they reconcile at the optimum — ~100-200 runs per track ("hundreds, not thousands"). `R` is **fixed at 5 m**: the per-collision study takes the plate as one built object (§5); plate *sizing* is a vehicle mass-vs-capture trade, out of scope (§11). All footprint variation is carried by `r_foot/R`, the **shared** variable (sets column density Σ for the 1D track and `eta_capture` for the 2D track).

| Variable | Track | Range | Resolution |
|---|---|---|---|
| `v` | both (anchors) | 3.2-16 km/s | 3 anchors (3.2 / ~8 / 16) + dense 5-16 sweep — EOS dip located ≈ 0.57 near 11 km/s (ADR-0012, §10) |
| `rho_impact` | 1D | 0.16-0.64 kg/m^3 | 3-4 pts, confirmatory (expect pinned at the opacity floor; knee past τ≫1) |
| `L/D` | both | 0.3-3 | 4-5 pts (disk fails on pressure, cylinder wastes area) |
| `r_foot/R` | shared | 0.3-1.0 | 4 pts |
| divergence | 2D | 0-20° half-angle | 3 pts |
| curvature | 2D | flat / 0.10 / 0.15 depth-to-D | 3 discrete (ADR-0008) |
| `R` | fixed | 5 m | — |

The grid is a coarse scan to locate the feasible region and the knee; refinement is then targeted, not uniform — near the optimum, where the conservative floor dips below `f = 0.8` (ADR-0009/0013), **and around any surprising/anomalous result** the coarse scan turns up (refine locally to confirm it is physical, not a grid artifact). Density is the least-swept variable (constraint-pinned, `e_eff` saturates past the τ≫1 knee).

**Survivability frontier (local limits only):** for each shape, find the densest/sharpest cloud that keeps peak facesheet pressure under the SiC+Ti limit (baseline `P_limit = 400 MPa`, the conservative floor of the §5 band, swept to 700/900 at the 16 km/s anchor) and the reflected tensile/spall stress at the SiC-Ti interface under SiC spall strength (ADR-0011); record `f` there, and report ablation-per-pulse as the requirement back-propagated to the MEMS replenishment system rather than imposing it as a budget. The total-force/buffer-invariant leg is dropped (out of scope). Disk-like shots fail on pressure, over-concentrated cylinders fail on local burn-through and waste plate area, so the optimum is intermediate: an elongated, mildly diverging cloud fanned to roughly plate diameter on a shallow concave plate. *Landed (2026-06, Rung S; numbers corrected 2026-07):* the frontier is now computed and confirms this. Peak facesheet pressure is the reflected-shock stagnation pressure `≈ 1.2·ρv²`, measured from the 1D kernel's `peak_wall_pressure` (the physical EOS pressure of the wall cell; the coefficient is 1.20 at 11 km/s and 1.24 at 16 km/s, the `(γ_eff+1)/2` reflected-shock value — the originally reported `≈ 2.0·ρv²` from `peak_wall_force` was the artificial-viscosity first-impact spike, a numerical artifact; ADR-0010 correction). The `Σ = m/(π r_foot²) = ρL` contract maps each cloud shape to its density: a disk (small `L/D`) or tight footprint (small `r_foot/R`) packs the 25 kg into a short/narrow column → high `ρ` → high peak. **The `f`-maximizing corner (short disk + tight footprint) is the densest case and still fails catastrophically — ~1600 MPa at 16 km/s (with the concave local-peak focusing factor folded in)**, far over even the relaxed 900 MPa limit. But the useful-`f` story survives, relocated onto a moderate shape: with the corrected `c_stag` and the grid-converged 112×80 geometry sweep (Mach anchors 10/20), the **best *survivable* `f` is ≈ 0.78 at the transitional dip** (11 km/s, conservative `e_eff = 0.57`, 400 MPa baseline — just under the gate, within the ±0.03 numerics band), and **≈ 0.81 at 16 km/s** under the 400 MPa baseline (clears the gate; the relaxed 900 MPa limit adds almost nothing, ≈ 0.806, because the corrected peak pressure barely binds there). So survivability does not gut the result; it forecloses the unsurvivable high-`f` corner and lands the survivable optimum right at the `f = 0.8` line (ADR-0009).

**Wall-side cushions cannot relieve the peak (foreclosed).** A natural "soften the impact" idea — interpose a thin buffer-gas balloon, or lean on a thicker ablative spray, between the cloud and the plate so a high-`eta_capture` flat disk might survive — is foreclosed by momentum conservation. Peak relief requires spreading the impulse `∫P dt = (1+e_eff)·ρ·v·L` (the delivered momentum, i.e. the thrust) over a longer time, and a wall-side cushion does that only if its column density is comparable to the cloud's. A 0.01 atm balloon carries ~1% of the cloud's areal momentum (~100x too thin), so the cloud plows through it and still stagnates at the full `≈ 1.2·ρv²`; a cushion dense enough to matter is physically just *a longer cloud* (higher `L/D`) at a per-pulse mass/logistics cost. So the only peak-pressure lever is spreading the cloud's *own* arrival in time — the geometric `L/D` / footprint / shallow-concave trade above — and neither a buffer gas nor the ablator (a wall-loss/oxidation device that raises `e_eff`, ADR-0014, not a pressure device) is a survivability lever. See ADR-0024.

**Margin map over the scale knobs `R` and `m` (closed form, de-risking only).** Peak `≈ 1.2·ρv²` is *intensive* — blind to plate geometry (thickness, width-as-acreage) — so beyond cloud shape the only handles are the two **scale knobs**: plate radius `R` and pulse mass `m`. Both are analytic via the Σ contract (`ρ ∝ m/R³`, with `eta_capture(r_foot/R)` scale-invariant), so a wider plate or smaller pulse only relaxes the pressure ceiling by `headroom = (R/R₀)³·(m₀/m)`, admitting a denser/higher-`eta` shape and buying `f` back. This is a *pure rescaling* of the Rung-S frontier (`analysis.py --axis margin`, `make analysis-margin`), no kernel reruns. At 16 km/s/400 MPa (numbers corrected 2026-07) the pinned baseline `R=5 m, m=25 kg` already clears at **0.805**; headroom lifts it only to **0.822** at the 4.6× grid corner (`R=7 m, m=15 kg`), plateauing there — with the corrected (lower) peak pressure the baseline already survives its best shapes, so the margin gain is small (`~+0.02`) and the map's role is de-risking, not gate-crossing. (The dip: 0.777 → 0.792 across the same grid.) Both knobs are **pinned by external budgets** — `R` by vehicle dry mass (shrinking it is catastrophic, `1/R³`), `m` by per-pulse thrust × pulse-rate (a smaller pulse needs proportionally more pulses; `f` is scale-invariant in `m`) — so this is only the *`f`-side* of a system trade, recorded as a margin map (not pursued as a rung, no change to the deliverable `f(v)`). Plate *bending* is the separate, cloud-shape-invariant thrust-structure problem the truss backing handles (ADR-0011), not an `f` lever. See ADR-0010 (amendment).

**Dark-oil opacity seed (study point at the cool-to-low-transition window).** Seeding ~0.5% mass of a dark ablative oil into the cloud (distinct from the plate-surface dark ablative already in the paper) pyrolyzes to soot. A rough calc shows 0.5% soot raises the cool 3.2 km/s gas from tau ~0.05 to ~11-45 (a ~375x jump), flipping it from optically thin to thick. **The lever is bounded by soot's temperature ceiling, not by the loss budget** (ADR-0015): condensed carbon survives only to ~5000-5500 K at these stagnation pressures, so there is no soot at the transitional core (10-20 kK) or the 16 km/s end — and the high-v gas is already `tau >> 1` on its own, so the seed adds nothing there. Soot's real niche is the window where it survives *and* radiation is climbing: roughly `v <~ 5 km/s` (the cool anchor up to the low edge of the transition), plus trapping outward/sideways radiation as it passes through the cooler peripheral/upstream gas surrounding a hotter core. The study point asks: in that window, does seeding recover optical loss and improve `f`? It is **gated** on the loss decomposition showing the *escape-to-space* fraction (channel 1b, §6) — not the lumped optical fraction — is a meaningful part of the loss there, since the seed can only recover radiation headed outward, not the wall-directed half (ADR-0016). The two gate conditions are in tension: channel 1b rises with `v` while soot survival falls with it (ADR-0015), so the live region is the narrow ~4-5 km/s window. Where the gate opens, the study re-runs that anchor's 1D with the seed's Planck/Rosseland-mean opacity added to the table and reports `delta-e_eff`, `delta-f`, the recovered 1b fraction, and the residual, with the `E(m)` tau-range as the recovery error band; the seed appears only in the best-estimate curve, never the conservative floor (ADR-0013). The seed (channel 1b) and the ablating-wall vapor shield (channel 1a, ADR-0014) are complementary, each recovering the radiative half the other cannot. A 0.5% contamination is judged acceptable against the biocompatibility goal. (Family resemblance to the ablating wall's near-wall carbon absorber, ADR-0014: same Rayleigh-carbon physics, sourced in the bulk cloud rather than at the wall.)

**Soot-lifetime check (precondition on the seed model).** The opacity is only delivered if the soot forms in time and is not consumed before the pulse ends (~hundreds of us, ms-scale tail). The seed model carries two order-of-magnitude clocks against the pulse, not full kinetics: (1) *inception* — droplet flash-pyrolysis (oil -> soot) must complete before the rebound; (2) *consumption* — oxidation (Arrhenius/NSC or O-OH collision-limited rate against the equilibrium O/OH from the EOS table) plus sublimation must be slower than the pulse. Oxidation caps the usable window from the top: as `v` climbs toward the ~5000 K sublimation ceiling, water dissociates and floods the gas with O/OH, so the soot burns. The oxidation cap likely pulls the top of the usable window down to ~4 km/s, below the sublimation ceiling. **Feedstock: dark oil is baseline** (fine-droplet dispersion, biocompatibility, matches the paper's plate-surface ablative family); if the inception clock proves too slow, the fallback is a pre-sooted oil or a fast-sooting heavy aromatic, not raw carbon black (which gives instant opacity but disperses poorly — agglomerated soot has lower effective `tau` than well-mixed).

**Optical model and the structure of the tau-jump.** Soot primaries (~10-50 nm) are Rayleigh at the 1700 K Planck peak (`x = pi*d/lambda ~ 0.05`); aggregates are treated with **RDG-FA** — absorption is `N x` the primary (no aggregation enhancement), only scattering is enhanced, and since the thermal-IR single-scatter albedo is small the model is **absorption-dominated RDG with scattering folded into an effective extinction**, full Mie reserved for if aggregates reach `x -> 1`. Because `kappa_lambda ~ 1/lambda`, the seed enters the ADR-0007 table as a **Planck-mean** addition primarily (the cool optically-thin regime, ADR-0006), with a Rosseland-mean entry too. Two structural facts fall out of the tau-jump formula `tau = C_0 * Y * Sigma / (rho_soot * lambda)` with `C_0 = 6*pi*E(m) ~ 5`, `Sigma = m_pulse / A_footprint` (so `tau ~ 8*Sigma`, Sigma in kg/m^2): (1) **tau is set by *column* density, not volumetric density**, so it is independent of compression and **coupled to the footprint knob `r_foot/R`** — the same knob that sets local flux and pressure, so the seed lever and the survivability frontier share one variable; (2) the **11-45 range is genuine `E(m)` optical-constant uncertainty** (literature ~0.2-0.4, factor ~2) folded with the footprint span and Planck-weighting — a two-sided uncertainty that propagates into the best-estimate error bars (ADR-0013), not fuzziness to be collapsed to a point.

## 8. Tooling and implementation

### Architecture: factored, because tau >> 1 makes the radiation nearly 1D

A monolithic 2D rad-hydro swept across the frontier is impractical (hundreds of runs) and unnecessary: tau >> 1 makes radiation local-diffusive rather than a global transport problem, and the radiative-vs-hydro timescale competition is a 1D problem at the wall.

| Track | Tool | Produces |
|---|---|---|
| restitution + wall load | **1D Lagrangian rad-hydro** (equilibrium EOS, real opacity tables, flux-limited diffusion, rigid wall then ablating wall) | `e_eff`, peak flux, peak pressure vs (rho, pulse shape) |
| geometric capture | **2D axisymmetric Euler**, radiation-free (effective-gamma sweep, equilibrium-EOS spot-check) | `eta_capture` vs plate curvature and cloud shape |
| confirmation | **FLASH** (academic license) or **Castro** (fully open) at the optimum; **Athena++** for the `eta_capture` track (deferred) | independent cross-check |

The 2D track runs radiation-free because the gas is optically thick (energy stays in the gas) and the capture question is adiabatic geometry. Because `eta_capture` is a 2D/1D ratio (ADR-0003), EOS error is largely common-mode, so the sweep uses a calibrated effective-gamma with a full-equilibrium-EOS spot-check at the optimum (ADR-0008). Exception: a deep dish would couple radiation into the focus, which is one reason it was dropped.

**`J_wall^2D` extraction (operationalizes ADR-0001/0003/0008).** The control surface is the **plate surface itself**, axial projection: `J_wall^2D = ∫_t ∫_plate P(r,t)·(n̂·ẑ) dA dt` — the time-integrated axial pressure force (flat plate: `n̂·ẑ = 1`; shallow concave: only the axial projection counts, radial components cancel by axisymmetry). This is preferred over a far-field momentum-flux surface: it is the direct physical quantity (axial momentum delivered) and less error-prone, and it inherently captures both geometry losses `eta_capture` measures — gas escaping past the plate edge is never counted, and gas that lands but rebounds sideways contributes its landing impulse without an axial rebound bonus (both pure geometry, since the track is radiation-free and adiabatic). The integration window uses the **same `10⁻³` force-decay cutoff as ADR-0001**, applied to the spatially-integrated `F_z(t)`, cutoff sensitivity reported. **2D caveat:** a concave plate can focus the rebound into a *delayed secondary* `F_z` peak (axial re-impact), so integrate until `F_z` falls below `10⁻³` of the **global** peak *and stays there* — never stop in the trough between a primary and a focused secondary peak. The denominator `J_wall^1D` is the identical-EOS, radiation-free, adiabatic, same-total-mass, same-`v` lossless 1D run with the same cutoff (ADR-0008); `eta_capture = J_wall^2D / J_wall^1D` then isolates geometry alone. *Landed (2026-06)* in `euler2d`'s ghost-cell IBM (ADR-0023 amended), with a useful simplification for the curved surface: the axial force on a dished annulus is `Σ_r P(r)·(n̂·ẑ)·dA = Σ_r P(r)·r·dr` exactly — the arc-length factor `√(1+s²)` of the tilted `dA` cancels the `1/√(1+s²)` of `n̂·ẑ` (slope `s = dz_s/dr`), so the axial impulse is the surface pressure integrated over the *projected* annulus regardless of slope, and the sustained-below-cutoff window does capture the concave plate's focused secondary peak (it is not truncated in the trough).

### Implementation language: Rust core, Python at the table and analysis boundary

The Rust lean is correct, but the decisive reasons are kernel speed and a trustworthy from-scratch solver, **not** the GIL. The sweep is embarrassingly parallel (independent runs), and even in Python that parallelizes cleanly via multiprocessing, which dodges the GIL entirely; the GIL only bites in-process threading, which a parameter sweep does not need.

- **Rust for the hot path:** the 1D rad-hydro kernel, the 2D Euler kernel, and the sweep driver (rayon for data-parallel runs across all cores). This is where the hundreds of runs live. Rust gives machine speed, fearless parallelism, and a solver whose correctness you can defend.
- **Python for the cold path:** generate the EOS and opacity tables once as offline preprocessing — a regime-stitched pipeline producing a unified `(rho, T) -> (p, e, c_s, kappa_Rosseland, kappa_Planck)` table: CoolProp (low-T two-phase) stitched to CEA/Saha (high-T dissociation/ionization) for the EOS, and HITEMP/ExoMol (cool molecular) stitched to TOPS/OPLIB (plasma) plus a soot model for opacity (ADR-0007), and do all plotting, frontier extraction, and loss-decomposition analysis on the solver's output. This ecosystem is the real reason to keep Python in the loop, and it never touches the hot loop.
- **Boundary = a file format**, split by data kind: **HDF5 for the gridded EOS/opacity tables** (self-describing N-D arrays, chunked/compressed, carrying ADR-0007 per-regime provenance as HDF5 attributes so each table cites its own sources; the Rust kernel loads the whole table into RAM once and interpolates in-memory — no per-cell disk I/O in the hot loop) and **Parquet for the sweep results** (columnar, typed, one row per run: parameters + `e_eff`/`eta_capture`/`f` + the five loss channels of ADR-0016 + peak flux/pressure). CSV is for human-readable exports and small summaries only. Python writes tables (`h5py`) and reads results (`pyarrow`/pandas); Rust reads tables (`hdf5` crate) and writes results (`arrow`/`parquet`). No FFI or PyO3 binding needed unless later desired.

Two worries answered directly:

- *Do we need Python optimization libraries?* No. The "optimization" is a grid sweep plus knee-finding (the problem is constraint-dominated and `f` saturates near 1, so there is no smooth interior maximum to chase). Even if a real optimizer is wanted later, it is a cheap outer loop wrapping the expensive kernel, so its language is almost irrelevant. Rust has `argmin` if needed.
- *Are there Python hydrocodes we should use?* None fit this problem. Custom EOS, opacity, ablating-wall, and condensation models mean you write the kernel yourself regardless, so Rust is the better choice for it. (PyClaw/Clawpack and astro teaching codes exist but would all need to be rebuilt for this physics.)

**Optional de-risking path:** prototype the 1D kernel in NumPy first to pass the Sod / Marshak / elastic-limit smoke tests (§10), then port the validated kernel to Rust for the production sweep. This avoids debugging the physics and the borrow checker at the same time. Going Rust-first with `ndarray` is fine if preferred.

## 9. Validation and verification

**Deferred (frontier first), with a live smoke test.** The decision was to map the frontier first and do full V&V after, but the two analytic momentum limits are kept as a continuous smoke test during development: `f -> 1` for a lossless elastic wall and `f -> 0.5` for an inelastic stick. They cost nothing and catch gross hydro bugs. (The `f -> 1` limit is a solver-bookkeeping check using an idealized reflecting boundary, not a physical-gas target: a real re-expanding gas has `e_eff < 1` even with zero losses, since the rebound is a rarefaction fan with a velocity spread — by Cauchy-Schwarz `p_rebound < p_in`. The lossless gas-dynamic `e_eff` is the true bounce ceiling.) **A *preliminary* `f` is quotable externally (white paper) ahead of the validation below, framed as a single-code conservative-floor result with the cross-check named as outstanding — ADR-0009 (2026-06 amendment); the validation upgrades it from *preliminary* to *validated*.** The deliverable is **two curves** (ADR-0013): a *conservative lower-bound* `f(v)` with every one-sided approximation stacked pessimistically — the headline, defensible floor, quotable as soon as the smoke tests and Orion validation pass — and a *best-estimate* `f(v)` with error bars from a one-at-a-time sensitivity study over the genuine two-sided uncertainties. The gated refinements (ADR-0004/0006/0008/0012) are pulled only where the conservative floor dips below the `f = 0.8` line (ADR-0009).

- **Verification (solver solves its equations):** Sod, Noh, Sedov for the Euler track; a Marshak wave for the flux-limited diffusion; the `f -> 1` / `f -> 0.5` momentum limits.
- **Validation (physics is right), keystone = Orion:** reproduce Project Orion's published impulse-per-pulse and ablation-per-pulse from their stated inputs (Balcomb 1970, already cited in the paper). The entire `f` argument descends from Orion's opacity finding, so reproducing their numbers and then extrapolating the same code to PuffSat conditions (lower-Z, lower-T, ~1/5 the velocity) is the §3.2 argument made quantitative. No other single check buys as much credibility.
- **Cross-code (split by leg):** the `eta_capture` (radiation-free 2D) track is cross-checked with **Athena++** (fully open, no registration, turnkey hydro). Radiation/EOS/opacity correctness rests on the Orion keystone, the Marshak verification, and table provenance — stronger than a sim-vs-sim comparison. Only the *combined* 2D rad-hydro at the optimum (the factorization check) wants a FLASH-class code, and it is the **least load-bearing leg**: `tau >> 1` bounds the factorization error. For that single point, **FLASH** (no-cost academic license) is primary; **Castro** (open multigroup flux-limited diffusion, BSD) is the fully-open fallback, accepting shared EOS/opacity tables guarded by provenance. (Quokka — open, two-moment transport — only if an independent radiation *model*, not just an independent implementation, is wanted.)
- **Provenance:** opacity from citable sources, split by regime and never hand-rolled — **HITEMP/ExoMol** (cool molecular water-vapor bands, low-v) and **TOPS/OPLIB** (ionized plasma, high-v), plus a soot Mie/Rayleigh model for the dark-oil seed. EOS from CoolProp (low-T two-phase) stitched C1-continuously to CEA/Saha (high-T dissociation/ionization). See ADR-0007.

## 10. Build order (rungs)

- **A. 1D ideal-gas smoke test.** Lagrangian hydro, effective-gamma EOS. Pass Sod and the `f -> 1` / `f -> 0.5` limits. (Built straight to Rust, test-first: the analytic tests — exact-Sod, convergence, the two momentum limits — *are* the physics-debugging harness, so no throwaway NumPy prototype/port. See `CLAUDE.md`.)
- **B. 1D high-v package.** Add equilibrium EOS + flux-limited diffusion + rigid wall; produce `e_eff(rho)` at 16 km/s. *Landed (2026-06):* a from-scratch analytic equilibrium water EOS + FLD + rigid wall (radiative channels) + an **interim bracketing opacity**; `e_eff(rho) ≈ 0.63` (rising 0.629→0.636 over `rho = 0.16→0.64`). `e_eff` is opacity/loss-insensitive — confirmed by a 0.1x/10x opacity scan (≤1.6 %) and a no-conduction comparison (0.63 vs 0.64 lossless) — so the two opacity/conduction-dependent outputs split off to **B-flux**.
- **B-flux. 1D high-v wall flux + survivability (follow-on to B).** The plate heat load — radiative-to-plate (1a) and conductive-to-plate (2) — and the peak-pressure/flux survivability check vs `P_limit` (§7, ADR-0010/0011). Deferred from B because `e_eff` does not depend on it, and because both halves need work B did not: the **real per-regime opacity table** (ADR-0007) for 1a, and a **gas-side conduction operator** for 2 — the inviscid Euler+AV kernel (ADR-0022) has no gas-side thermal resistance, so coupling the cold semi-infinite plate (ADR-0005) over-drains the thin wall gas cell. *Conduction half landed (2026-06):* a **combined gas+solid backward-Euler conduction operator** (`Solid::step_coupled`, one tridiagonal over the union; gas conductivity `k_gas` from CoolProp transport, ADR-0005/0007), verified against the two-semi-infinite-media contact analytic (effusivity-weighted interface `T_i`, erf each side) + order-of-accuracy + energy closure. The interface temperature now emerges from flux continuity, so the over-drain cannot recur (`CoupledBounce` is physical at high Mach). **Still deferred:** the high-v **plasma transport** `k_gas` (Spitzer-like — the high-v table carries no `k_gas`, so the 16 km/s `e_eff` pass keeps `wall = None`), the **real per-regime opacity table**, and the wall-flux/peak-pressure survivability report. The conduction operator unblocked the low-v worst-case question — see Rung C.
- **C. 1D low-v package.** Cool-gas EOS, optically thin, two-channel condensation (bulk two-phase EOS + wall sticking BC), equilibrium baseline at `α = 1`. Produce the worst case: water at 3.2 km/s. Kinetic condensation only if equilibrium fails the useful-`f` gate. *Landed (2026-06), as an adiabatic upper bound:* CoolProp two-phase EOS + a true mass-sink wall-sticking BC (radiation off, design §3). `e_eff(rho) ≈ 0.784 → 0.735` over `rho = 0.16→0.64`. **Finding — the low-v worst case is conduction-gated:** wall condensation is driven by the cold wall cooling the gas below `T_sat` (ADR-0004), i.e. by **conduction**, which is deferred to **B-flux**; without it the wall never condenses (wall-sticking loss ≈ 0) and the bulk condensation that does occur (~25 % in the cool re-expansion tail, captured by the EOS) sits in the low-pressure tail and barely dents `e_eff`. So in this adiabatic model 3.2 km/s bounces *better* than 16 km/s (0.74 vs 0.63); the condensation-dominated worst case the design anticipates only materializes once **B-flux** adds the wall-cooling conduction that drives deposition. The C-rung machinery (two-phase EOS, `liquid_frac` loader field, `CondensingBounce` sink) is built and verified, dormant until then. **Update (B-flux conduction added, 2026-06):** with the gas-side conduction operator now wired into `CondensingBounce` (a cold SiC-like plate, `k_gas` from CoolProp transport), the low-v conduction is **negligible** — channel 2 removes ≈ 0.15 % of the bounce energy, because over the ~µs bounce the thermal penetration into the (poorly-conducting) vapor is microns-thick and carries negligible mass. So it does **not** cool the bulk near-wall gas below `T_sat`, wall deposition stays ≈ 0, and `e_eff(ρ) ≈ 0.783 → 0.735` is **unchanged from the adiabatic bound** — that bound is now confirmed as the answer (within ~0.15 %). **Net: 3.2 km/s is *not* the worst case** — it bounces better than 16 km/s (0.74 vs 0.63) *with conduction on*, not just adiabatically. The condensation-dominated worst case the design anticipated does not appear at 3.2 km/s in 1D: bulk condensation sits in the low-pressure tail and wall deposition is starved by the µs timescale. (The wall gas cell is far coarser than the microns-thick thermal layer, so the model reports deposition as *exactly* 0; the physical value is ~0 — the cooled layer's mass is negligible regardless of resolution, so `e_eff` is mesh-robust.)
- **Transitional anchor (follow-on to B/C, ADR-0012).** Rungs C and B-flux showed neither endpoint is the floor (3.2 km/s bounces *better* than 16 km/s), so the worst case is **located by measurement** in the partial-ionization window, from the side computable now. *Landed (2026-06):* a `--transitional` velocity sweep (`V_GRID` = 5–16 km/s × `RHO_GRID`) reusing the high-v `water.json` and emitting **two curves per `(v, ρ)`** — **EOS-only** (`run_bounce`, radiation+conduction off: the opacity-independent dissociation/ionization specific-heat feature) and **radiation-on** (`CoupledBounce`, interim opacity, `wall = None`: the 16 km/s pass's config) — with a velocity-axis analysis (`analysis.py --axis v`) that locates the dip and reports the EOS↔rad gap. **Result — there *is* an EOS-driven dip below both endpoints:** the ρ-mean EOS-only `e_eff(v)` falls from ≈ 0.70 (5 km/s) to a **minimum ≈ 0.57 near 11 km/s**, then recovers to ≈ 0.64 at 16 km/s — well under the 0.74 (3.2 km/s) and 0.64 (16 km/s) anchors. So the floor of `f(v)` is the transitional dip, as ADR-0012 anticipated; the dip velocity (~11 km/s) sits at/above the a-priori 5–9 km/s window (energy sinking into dissociation/ionization is the mechanism, and it bottoms where the chemistry is most active). The radiation-on curve tracks it only ≈ 0.004 below (the interim-opacity band), **but that interim opacity is structurally wrong at `τ ~ 1`** (Kramers `κ∝T⁻³·⁵` gives `τ ≫ 1`), so the *radiative* `τ~1` leak dip is **not** resolved here — it needs the real per-regime opacity table (the deferred B-flux sibling). **Decision:** the EOS-only floor (≈ 0.57) is the trustworthy worst-case-so-far and a lower bound on `f`'s EOS side; the real-opacity rung is needed only to quantify how much deeper the radiative leak pushes it — so if ≈ 0.57 already clears a useful `f`, that rung is a refinement, not a gate.
- **D. 2D Euler geometry.** `eta_capture` for flat and shallow-concave plates vs cloud shape. *Kernel + flat plate landed (2026-06):* a from-scratch **2D axisymmetric Euler kernel** (`crates/euler2d`, ADR-0023) — HLLC Godunov, second-order MUSCL-Hancock, Strang dimensional splitting, and the conservative cylindrical `(1/r)∂(rF_r)` source + `p/r` — verified against the Sod tube (embedded in 2D), the cylindrical **Sedov** blast and **Noh** implosion (the axisymmetric source), and a smooth-flow **order-of-accuracy** test (≈ 2nd order). `eta_capture` is the lossless 2D/1D wall-impulse ratio (ADR-0003), formed from two runs of this kernel — a **confined** plane-wave run (the collimated 1D limit, cross-checked against the independent 1D `hydro1d` kernel to ~5 % on `1+e_eff`) divides out the common re-expansion, leaving pure geometry. **Result — the flat-plate `eta_capture` floor rises ≈ 0.81 → 0.92 over footprint `r_foot/L = 0.5 → 2`** (M ≈ 5; wider, disk-like clouds re-collimate toward the 1D ceiling of 1, narrow ones splat — flat is the hemispherical-rebound floor, ADR-0021). Illustratively, the transitional worst-case `e_eff ≈ 0.57` with a representative flat-plate `eta_capture ≈ 0.87` gives a first `f = eta_capture·(1+e_eff)/2 ≈ 0.68` bracket — a floor that shallow concave can only raise. *Shallow-concave plate + parametric sweep + first `f` bracket landed (2026-06):* a **ghost-cell immersed boundary** (true-normal mirror, ADR-0023 amended) adds the dished reflecting surface `z_s(r) = depth·(r/r_plate)²` to `euler2d` without disturbing the verified square-grid sweeps; flat and concave both run *through* the IBM (apples-to-apples), gated by a consistency test tying the IBM flat wall to the verified grid-aligned flat `eta_capture` (rel < 0.10). The parametric **curvature × `L/D` × `r_foot/R` sweep** (`crates/sweep --geometry`, two Mach anchors) + the Python **`f`-reconciliation** (`analysis.py --axis geometry`, the `Σ = m/(π r_foot²)` contract → `sigma_over_rho = 2·(L/D)`, ADR-0003) then close the loop. **Result — shallow concave lifts `eta_capture` above the flat floor and rising with depth:** at a short disk-like cloud (`L/D = 0.3`, `r_foot/R = 0.3`, M ≈ 5) `eta_capture` rises **0.88 → 1.02 → 1.03** over `d/D = 0 / 0.10 / 0.15` (concave *over-collimates* past the plane-wave 1D limit — bending the rebound more axial than a flat plane wave — so `eta_capture > 1` is physical, not an artifact). End-to-end the **`f`-floor clears the useful gate where the cloud need not be stretched:** at the transitional dip `e_eff = 0.57` the flat floor reaches `f ≈ 0.696` and shallow concave **`f ≈ 0.83` (> the 0.8 gate, ADR-0009)**; at 16 km/s (`e_eff = 0.63`) concave reaches `f ≈ 0.86`. **Deferred to a later rung:** the full `Σ`-resolved `e_eff(ρ(r_foot))` lookup (the dual-curve `f(v)` deliverable, ADR-0013) and the **equilibrium-EOS spot-check** (ADR-0008).
- **E. Ablating wall + levers.** Phase-2 passive ablating wall (quasi-steady effective-`Q*` surface energy balance; vapor injected as a wall mass/momentum/energy source — blowing cuts conductive loss, vapor opacity shields radiation, injected mass joins the bounce), the best-estimate counterpart to the rigid-wall conservative floor and the candidate lever to fill the transitional `e_eff` dip (ADR-0014, ADR-0012). Plus the dark-oil opacity-seed study; the pressure-limit sensitivity sweep. *Landed (2026-06):* the **`AblatingBounce`** kernel (1D Lagrangian, `wall = None` — the realistic high-v config) adds three sub-steps to the rigid `CoupledBounce`, each verified against its off-limit: a **quasi-steady `Q*` surface energy balance** with a wall **mass source** `ṁ = q_in/Q*` (`Q* → ∞` recovers the rigid floor; mass/energy closure holds), a **blowing factor** `φ = 1/(1+B)` on the conductive flux (`φ → 1` recovers the bare flux; **null at both anchors** — the high-v table carries no `k_gas` and low-v conduction is negligible — so blowing is a verify-and-bound channel, not a live lever), and a **vapor shield** via a new `RadBc::MarshakAttenuated` that throttles the wall radiative conductance by `1/(1+τ_v)`, `τ_v = κ_vapor·m_ablated`, **retaining the intercepted radiation in the near-wall field self-consistently** (`κ_vapor → 0` recovers the bare wall; shielding monotonically cuts `loss_radiative_wall`, and the recovery tracks the recoverable loss — the τ-leverage). The **`--ablating` sweep** (two anchors × `ρ` × `Q* ∈ {2,5,10} MJ/kg` × an opacity-scale knob `Table::with_opacity_scale` spanning τ) + **`analysis.py --axis ablating`** report the recovery as a **τ-bracket** against the rigid floor, folding in the Σ→ρ→peak survivability frontier (Rung S). **Result — the recovery is real but small, and *injection-dominated*** (refining ADR-0014's "shielding is the lever"): at 16 km/s the transparent floor (no shielding) is already `+0.017…+0.028` of pure mass-injection recovery, with the shielding τ-leverage adding only `~+0.017` on top. **Dip (11 km/s):** ablating lifts `e_eff` from the radiation-on rigid ≈ 0.566 only to **[0.570, 0.580]** (recovery ≤ +0.014) — the 0.57 worst case is an **EOS dissociation specific-heat sink, not a radiative loss**, so the shield has almost nothing to claw back: **the dip is not radiatively fillable** (`0.57` is `e_eff`, *not* `f` — the dip still clears a useful **`f ≈ 0.80`** via the concave `eta_capture` lever, Rung D; the wall is simply not the tool for it). **16 km/s:** `e_eff` 0.629 → **[0.638, 0.678]**; best survivable `f` 0.784 → **[0.788, 0.807]** — the `f = 0.8` gate **straddles** the bracket: it clears only at the optimistic low-`Q*`/high-τ corner (`f` 0.807, but ablating **~9 % of the plate** at `Q* = 2 MJ/kg`, straining the thin-curtain assumption and plate durability); the conservative `Q* = 10 MJ/kg` end lands 0.788, just under. Relaxed to the 900 MPa survivability limit it clears comfortably (0.859). **So the ablating wall does *not* robustly lift 16 km/s over the useful gate** (the user-deferred recovery-lever decision) — it is a marginal, `Q*`/durability-limited clear, decisive only if the structure tolerates 900 MPa. **E5 (real per-regime opacity, gated) not pulled:** the decision is `Q*`/EOS-limited, not τ-limited (real opacity sharpens only the ~38 % shielding sub-component and cannot fill the EOS dip), *and* the data (HITEMP/ExoMol/TOPS/OPLIB) is firewall-blocked by default-deny — so the τ-bracket is the deliverable and the point estimate defers to Rung F. The **dark-oil opacity-seed study** stays coupled to E5's soot infrastructure (gated); the **`P_limit` sensitivity** was swept in Rung S and is folded into the survivable-shape check here, not a separate slice.
- **F. Validation.** Cross-code split by leg: **Athena++** for `eta_capture`, **FLASH** (or open fallback **Castro**) for the single combined 2D rad-hydro factorization check at the optimum. **Orion** impulse/ablation reproduction is the physics keystone. Only now is `f(v)` quotable.

## 11. Out of scope

- **Total-performance / vehicle-acceleration analysis** (the buffer invariant `m*s ~= M a T^2/4` and its inputs: vehicle mass, target acceleration, pulse cadence, absorber stroke). `f(v)` is a per-collision property reusable regardless of how pulses are smoothed into motion.
- **Spatially-varying `f` across the plate** (off-center hits load the RCS). The first pass takes `eta_capture` as a footprint-averaged quantity.
- **Near-Sun regime** (hundreds of km/s), which the paper handles with solid projectiles and a separate chamber.
- **Composition sweep beyond water + the O2/ANFO spot-checks.**
- **Multi-pulse plate thermal accumulation between shots** (depends on cadence, which is a performance input).
- **Atomic displacement / sputtering / penetration damage to the facesheet crystal.** Per-atom energy is eV-scale, at or below the SiC displacement threshold across the envelope (ADR-0010); the facesheet threats are chemical (oxidation) and thermal (shock), not atomic.
- **Rayleigh–Taylor / Richtmyer–Meshkov mixing at the near-wall boundary layer** (the Orion follow-on concern). `e_eff` is a momentum ratio (ADR-0001), and RT corrugates the interface without creating or destroying axial momentum, so it cannot move the bulk impulse — only the conductive-to-plate loss channel, one-sidedly. It is unmodeled (the 1D track cannot represent it), gated on a channel-2 watchdog: if conductive-to-plate is ever a material slice of `(1−f)`, that anchor gets a bounding turbulent-conduction correction before `f` is quoted. RT also makes the ablating-wall vapor shield (ADR-0014) an upper estimate and the ablation-per-pulse requirement an underestimate. See ADR-0020.
